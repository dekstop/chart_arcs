Chart Arcs

A simple visualization of music listening behavior: How frequently do we change our listening habits?

  • Circles represent chart positions
  • Arcs represent chart movements

Circle size corresponds to the accumulated number of consecutive weeks that artists remained on this chart position.

Circle color signifies chart entry- and exit-points: The green color component of a circle corresponds to the number of times this chart position was an entry point for a rising artist, and the red color component the number of times this chart position was an exit point for a falling artist.

Arcs are directional and drawn clockwise: In the left half of a graph they point from bottom to top, in the right half from top to bottom. Arc strength corresponds to the number of times a chart movement occurred from the source to the target.
